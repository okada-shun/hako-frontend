Hako Finance Docs

Abstract

Welcome to Hako Finance!
Hako Finance is a DeFi application. This app makes it possible for user to transfer, get, lend, borrow and create valuable token.

Hako Finance application has three components, Hako, hako token and hako member.
Hako is a smart contract deployed to ethereum network. Hako token, or token, is a token used in this app. Hako member, or member, is an account to join Hako Finance system. You can turn your account into hako member account in "join Hako" transaction. By using member account, you can do various transactions.

Please see GitHub docs for more details.

Hako Information

In the following, I write items' descriptions in Hako Information.

Hako's address

This is the smart contract's address deployed to ethereum network. This contract is called "Hako" in this app and this contract's address is Hako's address.

Hako's balance of token

This is balance of hako token owned by hako contract. If hako member deposits his token to Hako, the token value is added to this hako's balance.

Total supply of token

This is the amount of total supply of hako token. It is equal to the sum of hako's balance of token and total balances of token owned by all accounts.

Hako's credit to Members

This is the value of hako contract's credit to hako members. It is equal to the total debts of all member accounts. About member's debt, please see here.

Hako's debt to Members

This is the value of hako contract's debt to hako members. It is equal to the total credits of all member accounts. About member's credit, please see here.

Member count

This is the number of all hako members' accounts.

Upper limit

This is the upper limit of credit value that hako member can borrow and create. Only hako owner can change it.

User Information

In the following, I write items' descriptions in User Information.

Your address

This is your own account's address.

balance of token

This is balance of hako token owned by your account. You can transfer your token to other account in "transfer token" transaction.

credit

This is the value of your credit to Hako. You can get it by depositing your token to Hako in "join Hako" or "deposit token" transaction. You can transfer your credit to other member account in "transfer credit" transaction and you can lend it in "lend credit" transaction. You can even create credit in return for owing equivalent debt in "credit creation" transaction.

debt

This is the value of your debt to Hako. You come to owe it if you can't return your debt to creditor member or if you create credit in "credit creation" transaction. If you owe debt, you become unable to withdraw your token from Hako, unable to borrow credit, unable to create credit and unable to get credit (in "withdraw token", "lend credit", "credit creation", "transfer credit" transaction). You can reduce your debt in return for reducing equivalent credit in "arrangement debt" transaction.

lending

This is the value of your credit to other member account. You come to get it by lending your credit to other member in "lend credit" transaction.

borrowing

This is the value of your debt to other member account. You come to owe it by borrowing credit from other member in "lend credit" transaction.

net assets

This is the value of your net assets. It is equal to (balance of token + credit - debt + lending - borrowing). You can borrow and create credit within this net assets and within the upper limit.

borrow value

This is the maximum value of credit that you can borrow from other member. You can register this value in "register borrowing" transaction.

borrow duration

This is the minimum duration of lending that you can borrow credit from other member. You can register this duration in "register borrowing" transaction.

Transactions

In the following, I write items' descriptions in Transactions.

transfer token

This is the transaction that you transfer your token to other account.

transfer credit

This is the transaction that you transfer your credit to other member account.
(You can't transfer it to non-member account and to the account that has debt to Hako.)

join Hako

This is the transaction that you join Hako by depositing some token to Hako. By joining Hako, you can become hako member.
(If you are already hako member, you can't join twice.)

leave Hako

This is the transaction that you leave Hako.
(If you have debt, you are lending your credit or you are borrowing credit, you can't leave.)

deposit token

This is the transaction that you deposit your token to Hako. By deposting, you lose your token but you get equivalent credit. In other words, you translate your token into credit.

withdraw token

This is the transaction that you withdraw your token from Hako. By withdrawing, you lose your credit but you get equivalent token. In other words, you translate your credit into token. This is the opposite of deposit token.
(If you have debt or you are borrowing credit, you can't withdraw.)

register borrowing

This is the transaction that you register borrow value and borrow duration. When you borrow credit, the lender can lend less than the value of credit and the lender can lend credit for more than the duration.
(If you are borrowing credit, you can't change these value and duration.)

lend credit

This is the transaction that you lend your credit to other member account. The maximum value of credit that you can lend is the borrow value of borrower member and the minimum duration of lending that you can lend is the borrow duration of borrower member.
(You can't lend your credit to the account that has debt to Hako.)

collect debt

This is the transaction that you, the creditor, collect debt from the debtor member.
(You can't collect if the duration is yet to be passed.)

return debt

This is the transaction that you, the debtor, return debt to the creditor memebr.

credit creation

This is the transaction that you create credit by owing equivalent debt to Hako. By doing this, you can get credit but you owe equivalent debt to Hako.
(If you have debt to Hako, you can't do credit creation.)

arrangement debt

This is the transaction that you reduce your debt to Hako by reducing your credit. By doing this, you can reduce your debt to Hako but you reduce your equivalent credit. This is the opposite of credit creation.
